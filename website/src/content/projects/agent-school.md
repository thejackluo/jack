Most “agent” products still assume one human in a chat window. **Agent School** is aimed at how work really runs: **Slack**, email, calendars—and **agents in the same channels as people**. The headline bet is **Agent Slack**: a collaboration surface where humans and agents share threads, permissions, and context. On top of that we care about **multi-agent workflows** (handoffs, parallel steps, review) and **reliable tool use** so calls to APIs and internal systems are grounded, not guessed.

## Focus

- **Agent Slack** — Humans and agents in shared rooms, with clear roles and limitations.
- **Multi-agent workflows** — Orchestration patterns for real tasks: routing, co-generation, approval, and recovery when something fails.
- **Tooling you can trust** — Schemas, checks, and habits that make agent actions traceable.

## Why it matters

When tool calls are wrong, the cost is bad data and broken trust—not a bad paragraph. Agent School treats collaboration design and correct tool use as product work, not extras.

## Public footprint

The [`agent-school`](https://github.com/agent-school) org is the home for **Agent Slack** and related repos. The site is [**agentschool.io**](https://agentschool.io). (A public **agentdex** index was discussed; when it ships, it will show up here as the registry for agents, tools, and evals.)

This is ongoing product and research: the bar is **multi-agent workflows** and shared surfaces that feel boringly reliable—not single-agent demos.
